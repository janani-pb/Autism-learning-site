Downloads and Age-Specific Information

Signs by Age Group

Infants (6-12 months)

  • Limited or no eye contact
  • Doesn't respond to name by 9 months
  • Lack of smiling or joyful expressions by 6 months
  • No back-and-forth sharing of sounds or facial expressions
  • Doesn't reach to be picked up
  • Unusual sensory responses (over/under sensitive)
  • Limited babbling or vocalizations
  • Doesn't point to objects by 12 months

Toddlers (1-3 years)

  • No single words by 16 months
  • No two-word phrases by 24 months
  • Loss of previously acquired language
  • Limited pretend or imaginative play
  • Preference for solitary play
  • Repetitive movements (hand flapping, rocking)
  • Intense focus on parts of objects
  • Difficulty with changes in routine
  • Unusual reactions to sensory input (textures, sounds)
  • Doesn't imitate actions or words

Preschool (3-5 years)

  • Delayed or unusual speech patterns
  • Echolalia (repeating words/phrases)
  • Difficulty understanding others' feelings
  • Unusual reactions to sensory input
  • Highly restricted interests
  • Difficulty with pretend play
  • Challenges making friends
  • Insistence on sameness/routines
  • Literal understanding of language
  • Unusual posture or movements
  • Extreme reactions to minor changes
  • Unusual attachment to objects

School Age (6-9 years)

  • Difficulty with social rules of conversation
  • Challenges with nonverbal communication
  • Intense, focused interests
  • Difficulty adapting to changes
  • Sensory sensitivities continue
  • Executive function challenges
  • May have advanced skills in specific areas
  • Difficulty understanding sarcasm or jokes
  • Struggles with group activities
  • Unusual speech patterns (monotone, too loud/soft)
  • Difficulty with organizational skills
  • May develop anxiety about social situations
  • Perfectionism about specific interests
  • Unusual responses to pain or temperature
  • Difficulty with team sports or cooperative play

Note: These signs don't necessarily indicate autism. Consult with a healthcare professional for evaluation.
