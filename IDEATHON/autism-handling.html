Recognizing Autism Signs

Common Early Signs

  • Social differences: Little eye contact, doesn't respond to name by 12 months
  • Communication delays: No single words by 16 months or phrases by 24 months
  • Repetitive behaviors: Hand-flapping, rocking, or spinning objects
  • Sensory sensitivities: Overreacts to sounds, textures, or lights
  • Routine dependence: Extreme distress at small changes
Signs usually appear by age 2-3, but may be seen earlier.

How to Support Autistic Individuals

Communication Tips

Use clear, concrete language. Allow extra processing time (5-10 seconds). Visual aids help immensely.

Meltdown Prevention

Identify triggers, maintain routines, provide quiet spaces. Never punish meltdowns - they're not tantrums.

Sensory Support

Offer noise-canceling headphones, textured fidget toys, or weighted blankets based on individual needs.
