Understanding Autism Spectrum Disorder

What is Autism?

Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) is a developmental condition that affects how people communicate, interact, and experience the world around them.

Key Characteristics:

  • Social differences - May prefer playing alone or have difficulty with conversations
  • Sensory sensitivities - May be over- or under-sensitive to sounds, lights, textures
  • Repetitive behaviors - May have specific routines or movements
  • Unique strengths - Often have exceptional memory, attention to detail, or creative thinking
Autism is a spectrum, meaning every autistic person has unique strengths and challenges.

Levels of Autism Support Needs

Level 1: Requiring Support

"Mild" Autism

  • Can speak in full sentences but may struggle with back-and-forth conversations
  • May appear socially awkward or have trouble making friends
  • Needs help with organization and planning
  • Example: A child who does well in school but has meltdowns when routines change
Level 2: Requiring Substantial Support

"Moderate" Autism

  • Uses simpler phrases or may be nonverbal at times
  • Has more obvious social difficulties even with support
  • Struggles with changes and needs daily help
  • Example: A teen who uses some words but primarily communicates with pictures
Level 3: Requiring Very Substantial Support

"Severe" Autism

  • Minimal verbal communication (may use sounds or gestures)
  • Needs constant supervision and support
  • Extreme difficulty with changes to routine
  • Example: An adult who needs help with daily tasks like dressing and eating

Remember:

These levels describe support needs, not intelligence or potential. With the right support, all autistic individuals can learn and grow.
